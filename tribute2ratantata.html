About Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. Born on December 28, 1937, he led the Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, and again as interim chairman from 2016 to 2017.

Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded significantly, acquiring international brands like Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus. His vision turned Tata into a global business powerhouse.

He is known not only for his business acumen but also for his simplicity, humility, and deep involvement in philanthropic efforts.

Key Milestones

  • 1937: Born in Mumbai, India
  • 1962: Graduated from Cornell University in Architecture
  • 1991: Became chairman of Tata Group
  • 2008: Acquisition of Jaguar-Land Rover
  • 2012: Retired as chairman of Tata Sons
  • 2016: Returned as interim chairman
  • Honored with Padma Bhushan (2000) and Padma Vibhushan (2008)
